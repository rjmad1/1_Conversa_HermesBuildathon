**Conversa: A Solution for Audio-First, Human-Like AI Conversations**

Conversa is a no-code, enterprise SaaS platform that transforms meeting **audio** and pasted or imported **transcripts** into interactive, conversational experiences powered by artificial intelligence. Conversa enables organizations to collect meeting audio, create and train AI interactions, and publish anywhere on the web, allowing audiences to ask questions and receive tailored responses in real time. This solution sits at the intersection of **speech processing**, natural language processing, and AI dialog management, delivering conversational AI experiences built on voice and text rather than video.

At its core, Conversa addresses the limitations of traditional note-taking, chatbots, and static FAQs. Rather than forcing users to read a linear transcript or scroll through a list of answers, Conversa lets them engage with recorded experts, trainers, or brand representatives as if they were in a live conversation. When a user asks a question, the platform retrieves the most relevant captured response from the meeting audio and transcript, creating an asynchronous yet humanized interaction. This approach preserves the authenticity and nuance of real human communication while scaling it to thousands of simultaneous users.

Conversa is designed for businesses and institutions that need to connect, train, and inform at scale without sacrificing personalization. Typical use cases include corporate training and onboarding, online education and asynchronous learning, customer service and FAQs, recruitment and sales enablement, and meet-and-greets with company leadership. For example, a global enterprise can record a single onboarding session with leadership and then let employees across regions and time zones ask their own questions, receiving context-aware answers instead of generic responses. In customer service, Conversa can replace static FAQ pages with interactive **audio** guides that answer product questions, troubleshooting steps, or policy clarifications in a more engaging format.

The platform's technical architecture combines **audio capture and ingestion**, AI question generation, transcription, translation, natural language processing, and publishing workflows into a single cloud-based web app. This makes it possible for non-technical teams to create interactive conversational experiences without coding or specialized AI expertise. Once published, Conversa content can be embedded on websites, learning platforms, or integrated into other applications, supporting a wide range of devices including smartphones, tablets, and desktops. Multi-language support further extends its reach, allowing organizations to serve global audiences with localized conversational experiences.

From a business perspective, Conversa offers several strategic advantages. It reduces the burden on human support and training resources by automating repetitive interactions while maintaining the feel of a human conversation. It improves engagement and retention by making learning and information discovery more interactive and agency-driven. It also provides analytics and insights on user questions, interaction patterns, and content performance, helping organizations continuously refine their messaging and training.

In summary, Conversa is a solution that bridges the gap between the scalability of AI and the trust-building power of human communication. By converting **meeting audio and transcripts** into interactive, conversational experiences, it enables organizations to connect with employees, customers, and prospects in ways that are immersive, personalized, and deeply engaging — moving beyond passive documentation to active dialogue.

### Scope Note (Audio-First)

This release is **audio-first**. Supported inputs are audio upload, recorded audio, pasted transcript, and imported transcript. **Video capture, video upload, video processing, video playback, avatar video, interactive video, and visual analysis are out of scope** in the current release (see `docs/adr/0002-audio-first-media-scope.md`). Interactive **video** experiences — where a recorded person answers questions on screen — are a **documented future capability**, not an implemented one; they would reuse the same ingestion → transcription → analysis pipeline behind a new media modality, without redesigning the core meeting and media domains.

**Builder note:** Implementable audio-first specifications live in `docs/` — start at `docs/INDEX.md`.
