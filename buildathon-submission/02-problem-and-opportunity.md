# Problem and Opportunity

## Problem
Teams leave meetings with decisions and action items, but execution often degrades because:
- Context is fragmented across transcripts, notes, tickets, and chat.
- Follow-up is manual and error-prone.
- Owners/dates are ambiguous or inconsistently captured.
- Governance is weak: AI can summarize, but enterprise teams still need explicit approvals and traceability.

## Pain Points
- PM/EM/Ops spend repetitive time converting meeting content to actionable work.
- Decision rationale is lost, reducing accountability.
- Cross-team execution lags due to ambiguous ownership and no audit view.

## Business Impact
- Slower cycle times.
- Lower action completion rates.
- Increased risk from untracked or unapproved actions.

## Current Alternatives
- Human-only notes and manual ticketing.
- Meeting assistants focused on transcripts/summaries but not governed action workflows.

## Opportunity Statement
A system that transforms meeting content into structured, reviewable actions with isolation and audit controls can reduce post-meeting operational overhead and improve execution reliability.

## Why Now
- LLM quality now supports practical extraction of decisions/risks/actions.
- Teams need AI acceleration with governance, not autonomous uncontrolled writes.

## Hypotheses (assumptions)
- H1: Teams accept AI-generated actions if approval remains human-controlled.
- H2: Structured meeting output improves follow-up consistency.
- H3: Isolation + auditability are required for enterprise adoption.

(Assumptions are not yet validated with production telemetry.)
